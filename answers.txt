Q3: What is the difference between Merging and Rebasing?

Answer: Merging combines changes from different branches into one branch, creating a new commit that preserves the history of both branches. Rebasing, on the other hand, moves or combines a sequence of commits to a new base commit, resulting in a linear history but potentially altering the commit history.
